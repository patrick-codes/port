Reflective Practice

Introduction

In this reflective document, I evaluate a lesson I conducted on the "Information Processing Cycle" at Akroso Senior High Technical School. The lesson was observed and assessed by mentors and supervisors, covering aspects such as preparation, delivery, strengths, areas needing improvement, challenges faced, innovative strategies utilized, and insights gained. This reflection aims to refine my teaching methods and continually improve my ability to effectively teach ICT concepts to students.

Download PDF Now

LESSON COVERAGE

  • School: Akroso Senior High Technical School

  • Department: ICT

  • Subject: Information and Communication Technology (ICT)

  • Topic: Information Processing Cycle

  • Class: SHS 1 (1H3)

  • Class Size: 52

  • Date: 19-01-2024

  • Duration: 60 minutes

INSTRUCTIONAL OBJECTIVES

  • Objective 1: Understanding Information Processing Cycle

  • Objective 2: Definition of Information

  • Objective 3: Group Activity and Presentation

PRESENTATION OF THE INFORMATION PROCESSING CYCLE LESSON

I initiated the lesson by introducing the Information Processing Cycle concept using visual aids and practical examples to ensure student comprehension.

CHALLENGES

During the lesson on the Information Processing Cycle, some students found it challenging to grasp concepts like data transformation stages, understand technical ICT terms, how devices are used in each processing stage practically.

INNOVATIVE SOLUTION

I implemented a classroom approach where students watch pre-recorded videos or interactive tutorials to familiarize themselves with the stages and technical terminology of the information processing cycle. These innovative approach leverage technologies to make learning more engaging, interactive, and relevant to students' understanding of the information processing cycle

LESSONS AND INSIGHTS GAINED

Teaching with clear visuals and real-world examples helps students understand better. Breaking down technical ICT terms and providing context helps students learn them well. Hands-on activities make it easier for students to apply what they learn about the information processing cycle. Working together in groups improves how students interact and understand the concepts
